How to onboard people to the War Room system

The Onboarding Sequence

Do NOT dump all 4 modules on someone at once. The system must be learned sequentially or it will not hold.

Week 1: System Reality & Drift (Lesson 1 only)

  • Have them complete Lesson 1 and the checkpoint
  • Discuss: "Describe one real situation where you compensated for drift instead of stopping"
  • If they cannot identify drift in their own work, they are not ready for the system
  • Stop here if they resist documentation or want "quick tips"

Week 2: Prime Directive + Framing Density (Lessons 2-3)

  • Lesson 2: AI generates, you execute, you document, reality decides
  • Lesson 3: The 4 mandatory framing layers (Identity, Constraints, Format, Verification)
  • Key test: Can they write a dense frame for their own problem with all 4 layers?
  • If they skip layers or treat framing as optional, repeat this week

Week 3: Memory Stacking (Lesson 4)

  • Copy → Store → Re-inject workflow
  • Mandatory 4-field stack format
  • One source of truth
  • Key test: Do they actually create a memory stack document and use it?

Week 4+: Advanced Modes (Module 2, optional)

  • Only introduce if they have mastered Lessons 1-4
  • Adversarial Stress Test, Ghost Protocol, Temporal Hierarchy, Execution Loop
  • Most people stop after Lesson 4. That's fine. Modules 1 is the foundation.

Team AI Policy Template

If you're implementing War Room protocols for a team or organization, your policy must define:

1. Who Can Use AI

  • Roles: which positions are authorized
  • Training requirement: completion of War Room Module 1 minimum
  • Seniority level: junior staff may have more restrictions

2. Approved vs. Prohibited Tasks

  • Approved: internal research, first drafts for review, brainstorming
  • Verification required: client-facing content, code to production, strategic recommendations
  • Prohibited: legal advice, medical decisions, final financial recommendations, safety-critical decisions

3. Required Documentation

  • Every AI-assisted task must log: task description, verification method, final decision-maker
  • Memory stack format for all decisions
  • Disclosure when AI was used in client-facing work

4. Review Cadence

  • Weekly: team check-in on AI usage and drift signals
  • Monthly: audit of AI-assisted work for quality and compliance
  • Quarterly: review and update policy based on failures

5. Accountability Assignment

  • AI generates. Humans execute, document, and are responsible.
  • Name who is accountable when AI-assisted work fails
  • Consequences for violation: warnings, re-training, access removal

Common Training Failures

"They want shortcuts, not discipline" — Then they are not ready. War Room is not for people seeking productivity hacks.

"They skip documentation" — Stop training. Documentation is non-negotiable.

"They resist checkpoints" — Checkpoints exist to prove understanding. No checkpoint completion = no progress.

"They want Module 2 before mastering Module 1" — Say no. Sequential completion is mandatory.
